About Me

"Zachary Laguna is a junior computer engineering student at Texas A&M University. He considers himself a forever student as he is eager to build upon his academic foundations in Computer Engineering and Mathematics in addition to staying in tune with the changing technological world around us."

The layman looks at a graphical interface to interact with their computers and never stops to question the development behind it. When I found out that computers only fundamentally use simple 0s and 1s, I was astonished and my curiosity in computational theory began to heighten. My first experience while coding was in high school where I learned to code in Java. Building my first few programs using Java gave me a glimpse into the infinite abyss of possibilities that could be attained through programming. I saw that programming was effective because it turns a simple task into a complex one. They are incredible at making processes more productive and efficient. Using Visual Studio Code, I created a rudimentary video game and a website. From building these programs I found that at the element of programming, true understanding, mathematics is fundamental. During that period, I realized that if I wanted to learn more I needed to dedicate my time to computing and mathematics which is what motivated me to apply for Computer Engineering at Texas A&M University.

Throughout my time at Texas A&M University, I have both attended and hosted lectures about computer science. Through these lectures, I learned about how people make sense of the world through science. It was though classwork that I realized that there were indecipherable problems that still haven't been solved. These problems seemed to be solvable through automation and simulation with future computing capability. My desire is to bring that future into the present.
